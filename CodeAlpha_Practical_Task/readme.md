# Extraction and Isolation of Genomic DNA from Plant Tissue

## Project Overview

This project is completed as part of the CodeAlpha Biotechnology Internship. The project focuses on the extraction and isolation of genomic DNA from plant tissue to understand the basic principles of molecular biology and biotechnology laboratory techniques.

The main objective of this project is to study the process of obtaining pure genomic DNA from plant samples and understand its importance in genetic analysis, research, and biotechnology applications.

## About Genomic DNA Extraction

Genomic DNA extraction is a fundamental technique in biotechnology used to isolate DNA from biological samples.

In plants, DNA is extracted from tissues such as leaves by breaking open plant cells, removing cellular components, and isolating purified DNA for further analysis.

## Principle of DNA Extraction

The process of genomic DNA extraction is based on the following principles:

1. **Cell Disruption**

Plant cells are broken open to release genetic material.

2. **Removal of Cellular Components**

Proteins, membranes, and other cellular materials are separated from DNA.

3. **DNA Purification**

The extracted DNA is purified to obtain a cleaner sample suitable for analysis.

## Extraction Process Overview

The general workflow includes:

### 1. Sample Preparation

Plant tissue is collected and prepared for DNA isolation.

### 2. Cell Lysis

Plant cells are disrupted to release DNA from the nucleus.

### 3. Separation of Impurities

Unwanted cellular components are removed.

### 4. DNA Isolation

The genomic DNA is separated and collected.

### 5. DNA Analysis

The quality and presence of extracted DNA can be evaluated using biotechnology techniques.
